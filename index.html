Measures of Central Tendency for Ungrouped Data

Mean:

Formula: X = ∑ given_number / total_number

X is the mean

Median:

Formula: Md = (total_number + 1) / 2

Md is the position and should always round up

Mode:

Unimodal - only one value that occurs with the greatest frequency

Bimodal - two values that are considered to be the mode

Multimodal - more than two values that are considered to be the mode

Midrange:

Formula: MR = (lowest value + highest value) / 2

MR is the midrange

MR is a rough estimate of the middle

Weighted Mean

Formula: X = ∑ (weight_of_given * given_number) / ∑ weight_of_given

A type of mean that is calculated by multiplying the weight (or probability) associated with a particular event with its associated quantitative outcome and then summing all the products together.

X = 21.5 / 11 = 1.95

Geometric Mean:

Formula: GM = √ (∑ given_numbers)

It is the average value or mean which signifies the central tendency of the set of numbers by finding the products of their values.

It is useful in finding the averagge of the percentage, rations, indexes, or growth rates.

Most useful when numbers in the series are not independent of each other or if numbers tend to make large fluctuations.
